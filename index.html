Guide

How to evaluate blue-chip crypto

What makes a crypto "blue chip"?

Blue-chip cryptos have survived multiple market cycles, have large and active developer communities, real-world adoption beyond speculation, deep liquidity, and are listed on all major exchanges. They're the crypto equivalents of S&P 500 companies — not risk-free, but the most battle-tested options available.

Technology and scalability

Evaluate each protocol's approach to the blockchain trilemma: decentralization, security, and scalability. Layer-1 chains like Solana and Avalanche prioritize speed, while Ethereum focuses on security and decentralization with Layer-2 scaling. There's no perfect solution — understand the tradeoffs.

Tokenomics matter

Look at total supply, inflation schedule, token utility, and staking yields. Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million creates scarcity. Ethereum's burn mechanism can make it deflationary. Inflationary tokens need strong demand to maintain value against dilution.

Building a portfolio

Most crypto-native investors use a core-satellite approach: 60-70% in Bitcoin and Ethereum as the core, with the remainder allocated to high-conviction Layer-1s and infrastructure tokens. Rebalance periodically and avoid chasing the latest narrative without understanding the fundamentals.
